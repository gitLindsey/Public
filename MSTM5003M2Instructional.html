Password Best Practices

  • Minimum length: 10-12 characters
  • Include uppercase and lowercase letters
  • Include numbers and special characters
  • Avoid personal information (names, dates)
  • Use unique passwords for each account
  • Enable password history to prevent reuse
  • Never write passwords down in plain text
Pro Tip: Consider using passphrases - longer combinations of words that are easier to remember but harder to crack!

Attack Scenarios

Practice identifying security threats in real-world scenarios

üìß Scenario 1: The Urgent Email

You receive an email from your "IT department" saying your account will be suspended in 2 hours unless you verify your credentials by clicking a link.

Threat Type: Phishing with Urgency

Red Flags: Time pressure, request for credentials, suspicious link

Proper Response: Contact IT directly through known channels to verify

üìû Scenario 2: The Help Desk Call

Someone calls claiming to be from IT support, asking for your password to "update system security patches remotely."

Threat Type: Vishing (Voice Phishing)

Red Flags: Unsolicited call, asking for passwords, creating false urgency

Proper Response: Hang up and call IT through official channels

üö™ Scenario 3: The Friendly Visitor

A person in a delivery uniform follows you through a security door, claiming they have a package for someone upstairs.

Threat Type: Tailgating/Piggybacking

Red Flags: Unauthorized access attempt, social engineering through helpfulness

Proper Response: Politely ask them to use proper entry procedures
