Privacy Fundamentals

Privacy is defined as the power to control what others know about you and what they can do with that information. In the digital age, this concept has become increasingly complex and critical.

Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
A set of elements that can lead to the specific identity of a person, requiring special protection to prevent misuse.
Data Sensitivity Labeling
Classification system for information including Public, Private, Confidential, and Proprietary categories.
Notice, Choice, and Consent
Three key principles governing ethical PII collection: informing users, providing options, and obtaining agreement.

U.S. Legal Framework

The United States has developed a comprehensive set of laws addressing privacy and computer security, each targeting specific sectors and types of data.

Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)
Criminalizes unauthorized access to computer systems involved in interstate commerce or national security.
Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
Protects medical information with security standards and privacy practices for healthcare data.
Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)
Requires financial institutions to explain information-sharing practices and protect customer data.
Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX)
Establishes controls for financial reporting systems, including IT security requirements.
